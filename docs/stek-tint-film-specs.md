## What actually differentiates the tiers

- All three look nearly identical from outside (same VLT, similar reflectance) — the difference is **heat**, not darkness. This is the core education point: "dark ≠ cool."
- The IR rejection gap is the big sellable number: Action 46% → Smart 79% → Nex 95% (780–2500 nm full IR band). This is the felt "skin heat" through the glass.
- TSER is the honest whole-film number: 50% → 56% → 61%. Use TSER for technical credibility, IR% for punchy ad copy.
- All lines are metal-free: zero interference with GPS, Salik tag, phone signal — a real UAE-specific selling point.
- Marketing descriptors from the book: Action = nano-carbon "fashionable and unfading"; Smart = nano-ceramic "never fades, unmatched clarity"; Nex = premium nano-ceramic "ultimate film, superb heat rejection."

## Ad-claim guardrails

- Say "blocks up to 95% of infrared heat" only for **Nex**. For Smart say "up to 79% IR rejection" or round down to "blocks ~70%+ of infrared heat."
- Never say "blocks 95% of heat" — IR ≠ total heat. Total heat rejected is the TSER figure (50–61%).
- "99% UV" is safe for all three lines.
- STEK USA site claims (SMART+ TSER up to 60%, NEX+ IR up to 94.59%) are for the plus-series and vary by shade — stick to the swatch-book numbers above for the films we actually stock.
- "Nano-ceramic" applies to Smart and Nex only. Action is carbon — don't call the AED 599 tier "ceramic."
